# Good vs Bad AI Scenarios

This document illustrates the difference between weak and strong AI task design.

---

## Example 1: Travel Booking

### ❌ Weak Scenario
"Book a flight tomorrow"

### Issues
- Missing key inputs
- No constraints
- Ambiguous execution

---

### ✅ Strong Scenario
"Book a flight from Boston to Chicago tomorrow between 6AM–10AM under $300 with preference for direct flights."

### Improvements
- Defined inputs
- Clear constraints
- Executable outcome

---

## Example 2: Content Generation

### ❌ Weak Scenario
"Create an Instagram post"

### Issues
- No audience definition
- No topic or tone
- No performance goal

---

### ✅ Strong Scenario
"Create an Instagram post targeting fitness enthusiasts aged 20–35, promoting a 30-day challenge with an engaging and motivational tone, including 5 relevant hashtags."

### Improvements
- Defined audience
- Clear objective
- Measurable output

---

## Example 3: AI Agent Workflow

### ❌ Weak Workflow
Research → Write → Publish

### Issues
- No validation stage
- No feedback loop
- No error handling

---

### ✅ Strong Workflow
Research → Validate → Generate → Review → Optimize → Publish

### Improvements
- Added validation step
- Introduced review cycle
- Improved reliability

---

## Key Takeaways

Strong AI scenarios:
- Define inputs clearly
- Include constraints
- Are executable
- Support reasoning
- Minimize hallucination risk

Weak scenarios:
- Are vague
- Lack structure
- Lead to inconsistent outputs

---

## Conclusion

High-quality AI systems depend on well-defined scenarios.

The difference between success and failure is often:
👉 **clarity + structure + evaluation rigor**
