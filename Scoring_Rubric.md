# AI Scenario Evaluation – Scoring Rubric

This rubric is used to objectively evaluate AI-generated scenarios, tasks, and workflows.

---

## Evaluation Dimensions

### 1. Logical Consistency
- Does the scenario follow a valid reasoning path?
- Are steps sequenced correctly?

**Score Guide:**
- 1 – Illogical or contradictory
- 3 – Partially consistent
- 5 – Fully logical and coherent

---

### 2. Context Completeness
- Are all required inputs defined?
- Are dependencies clearly specified?

**Score Guide:**
- 1 – Major gaps in context
- 3 – Some missing elements
- 5 – Fully defined and complete

---

### 3. Execution Feasibility
- Can the scenario be executed in a real system?
- Are constraints realistic?

**Score Guide:**
- 1 – Not executable
- 3 – Executable with assumptions
- 5 – Fully executable

---

### 4. Reasoning Depth
- Does the scenario test reasoning or just output generation?
- Are decision points meaningful?

**Score Guide:**
- 1 – Superficial
- 3 – Moderate reasoning
- 5 – Deep reasoning required

---

### 5. Edge Case Coverage
- Does the scenario consider variations or failures?
- Are boundary conditions addressed?

**Score Guide:**
- 1 – No edge cases
- 3 – Limited coverage
- 5 – Comprehensive coverage

---

### 6. Hallucination Risk
- Likelihood of incorrect or fabricated outputs
- Presence of unsupported assumptions

**Score Guide:**
- 1 – High hallucination risk
- 3 – Moderate risk
- 5 – Low risk (well grounded)

## Purpose

This rubric ensures:
- Objective evaluation
- Repeatable scoring
- Clear feedback for improvement

It aligns AI outputs with **real-world usability and reasoning integrity**.
